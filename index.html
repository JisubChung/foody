I grew up in the generation of Math Blaster and Reader Rabbit. DOS games were awesome and you had to “park” your hard drive to safely shut down your computer. My affinity for computers began as early as I could punch keys on the keyboard and see the letters pop up on the CRT monitor. I didn’t know how to type or code, but I saw my dad navigate through the hard drive and eventually was able to show off to my friends that I too could code by typing “dir” and list the current file directory tree - they were amazed.
When I wasn’t playing DOS games, I’d be doodling my favorite cartoon characters like Ninja Turtles, Yoshi, and Pochacco. Growing up, my folks rarely bought us gaming consoles to play with. So sometimes, I’d cut out my drawings and tell my brother to say forward, back, attack to defeat the other character cut out in a little game of Super-Smash-Joon-Sub’s-Cut-Out-Characters – he was never able to defeat the last boss. As times advanced, my uncle showed me a picture of him posing on the highway next to his bike and another stunning image of my tiny cousins climbing up a cup he was holding - incredible, I thought! It took me a while to realize that there were photo-editing applications beyond MS Paint – namely, Photoshop.
